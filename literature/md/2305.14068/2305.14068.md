## [Enriched string-net models and their excitations](https://quantum-journal.org/?s=Enriched%20string-net%20models%20and%20their%20excitations&reason=title-click)

David Green<sup>1</sup> , Peter Huston<sup>2</sup> , Kyle Kawagoe<sup>1</sup> , David Penneys<sup>1</sup> , Anup Poudel<sup>1</sup> , and Sean Sanford<sup>1</sup>

<sup>1</sup>The Ohio State University <sup>2</sup>Vanderbilt University March 6, 2024

> Boundaries of Walker-Wang models have been used to construct commuting projector models which realize chiral unitary modular tensor categories (UMTCs) as boundary excitations. Given a UMTC A representing the Witt class of an anomaly, the article [\[arXiv:2208.14018\]](http://arxiv.org/abs/2208.14018) gave a commuting projector model associated to an A-enriched unitary fusion category X on a 2D boundary of the 3D Walker-Wang model associated to A. That article claimed that the boundary excitations were given by the enriched center/M¨uger centralizer *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ) of A in *Z*(X ).

> In this article, we give a rigorous treatment of this 2D boundary model, and we verify this assertion using topological quantum field theory (TQFT) techniques, including skein modules and a certain semisimple algebra whose representation category describes boundary excitations. We also use TQFT techniques to show the 3D bulk point excitations of the Walker-Wang bulk are given by the M¨uger center *Z*2(A), and we construct bulk-to-boundary hopping operators *Z*2(A) → *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ) reflecting how the UMTC of boundary excitations *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ) is symmetric-braided enriched in *Z*2(A).

> This article also includes a self-contained comprehensive review of the Levin-Wen string net model from a unitary tensor category viewpoint, as opposed to the skeletal 6j symbol viewpoint.

## 1 Introduction

One of the most powerful features of topologically ordered phases of matter [\[Wen17\]](#page-44-0) is the robustness of their relevant properties under perturbation [\[Wen90,](#page-44-1) [WN90\]](#page-44-2). As an example, despite the fact that Laughlin's wavefunction [\[Lau83\]](#page-43-0) is not a precise solution to the problem of an electron gas in the presence of a magnetic field, it accurately captures the phenomena of fractionalized charge and fractionally quantized Hall conductance. Furthering this philosophy, Levin and Wen wrote down their famous string-net models [\[LW05\]](#page-44-3), which made concrete the connection between fusion categories and the physics they represent.

String net models condense topological order from the vacuum; indeed, a fusion category X is a condensable algebra in the symmetric monoidal 2-category 2Vect [\[GJF19,](#page-43-1) <span id="page-1-0"></span>[JFR24\]](#page-43-2). The procedure to condense X [\[Mul22\]](#page-44-4) corresponds to passing to the ground state space of the commuting projector Levin-Wen local Hamiltonian [\[LW05,](#page-44-3) [LLB21\]](#page-44-5), whose localized excitations can be described by the Drinfeld center *Z*(X ) [\[KKR10,](#page-43-3) [Kir11\]](#page-43-4), which is Witt-equivalent to the trivial topological order Vect [\[DMNO13\]](#page-42-0).

It is widely accepted that (2+1)D commuting projector models produce exactly those unitary modular tensor categories (UMTCs) which are Drinfeld centers as their categories of localized anyonic excitations. In particular, chiral UMTCs should only appear as boundaries of invertible (3+1)D topological quantum field theories (TQFTs) [\[JF22,](#page-43-5) [FT21\]](#page-42-1), which correspond to the anomaly [\[KT17\]](#page-43-6). This anomaly can be represented by a Witt-class [\[DMNO13\]](#page-42-0) of UMTCs [\[BJSS21\]](#page-42-2). This is a manifestation of a bulk-boundary correspondence; such correspondences appear throughout topological physics [\[Wen95,](#page-44-6) [Wen13,](#page-44-7) [Lev13,](#page-43-7) [KWZ17\]](#page-43-8).

To build a (3+1)D commuting projector model which realizes a chiral UMTC C as boundary excitations, we first fix a UMTC A representing the anomaly. We then choose an A-enriched unitary fusion category (X *,* Φ *Z* ), i.e. a unitary fusion category (UFC) X equipped with a unitary braided central functor Φ *Z* : A → *Z*(X ) [\[HBJP23,](#page-43-9) § II.B], so that the centralizer *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ) of Φ *Z* (A) in *Z*(X ) is C. The functor Φ *Z* is the data necessary to attach a (2+1)D Levin-Wen model [\[LW05\]](#page-44-3) for X as the boundary of a (3+1)D Walker-Wang model [\[WW12\]](#page-44-8) for A (in red below). We write Φ := *F* ◦ Φ *Z* : A → X , where *F* : *Z*(X ) → X is the forgetful functor.

The local Hamiltonian is made up of 1D edge terms and 2D plaquette terms for every 2D square in the lattice. The half-braiding for Φ(*a*) with X is used in a crucial way to define the plaquette terms at the interface of the 3D bulk with the 2D boundary. We refer the reader to §[3](#page-23-0) below for more details. We remark that related (2+1)D boundary theories for (3+1)D Walker-Wang models also appear in [\[BCFV14,](#page-42-3) [vKBS13\]](#page-44-9).

These A-enriched string net models condense topological order Witt equivalent to A from A-topological order; indeed an A-enriched fusion category is a condensable algebra in the fusion 2-category Mod(A) [\[D´ec23,](#page-42-4) Ex. 1.4.6]. Condensing X (cf. [\[Mul22\]](#page-44-4)) corresponds to passing to the ground state space of our A-enriched string net local Hamiltonian, whose localized excitations can be described by the enriched center *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ) := A′ ⊂ *Z*(X ) [\[KZ18\]](#page-43-10), which is Witt-equivalent to A [\[DMNO13\]](#page-42-0).

It was claimed in [\[HBJP23,](#page-43-9) § II.B] that the topological excitations which live on the boundary of the A-enriched string net model for X are described by the enriched center *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ), i.e., the M¨uger centralizer of A in *Z*(X ). We give a rigorous proof of this fact using TQFT techniques, including A-enriched skein modules for X and the dome algebra Dome<sup>A</sup> (X ), a certain quotient of the tube algebra Tube(X ) [\[Izu01,](#page-43-11) [M¨ug03\]](#page-44-10). In an ordinary (2+1)D string-net model, the algebra Tube(X ) acts as local operators near the site of a topological excitation, so that anyon types correspond to (isomorphism

classes of) indecomposable representations of Tube(X ). The presence of the (3+1)D A-bulk induces additional requirements on topological local operators, which cuts down Tube(X ) to Dome<sup>A</sup> (X ).

The structure of this paper is as follows. First, §[2](#page-3-0) is a self-contained primer on the Levin-Wen string net model [\[LW05\]](#page-44-3) from a unitary tensor category viewpoint. For simplicity, we use a square lattice, and all degrees of freedom occur at vertices, similar to [\[Kon14\]](#page-43-12). In §[2.1,](#page-3-1) we define the disk skein module associated to a UFC X , and prove basic results about skein modules which will be used throughout the paper. In §[2.2,](#page-6-0) we provide some helpful conceptual proofs of well-known facts in the literature, including the matrix coefficients for the plaquette operators in Lemma [2.8](#page-9-0) and the proof that the product of all plaquette operators is a scalar multiple of the projection to the skein module in Theorem [2.9.](#page-11-0) We also give an elegant description of the ground state space of the edge terms in terms of hom spaces in the UFC X in Proposition [2.6.](#page-6-1) In §[2.3,](#page-14-0) we review the basics of the tube algebra Tube(X ) and give the well-known correspondence between its category of representations and objects in *Z*(X ) [\[Izu01\]](#page-43-11). In §[2.4,](#page-17-0) we fully describe excitations localized on one edge and its two adjacent plaquettes, together with a shortened and simplified presentation of general string operators and their connection to Tube(X )-representations from [\[CGHP23\]](#page-42-5). Finally, in §[2.5,](#page-21-0) we give a shortened and simplified presentation of hopping operators from [\[CGHP23\]](#page-42-5).

Next, we analyze the A-enriched string net model for an A-enriched UFC introduced in [\[HBJP23,](#page-43-9) II.B] in §[3.](#page-23-0) We do so in more generality, assuming that A is unitary braided (but not necessarily modular), but still requiring Φ *Z* : A → *Z*(X ) to be fully faithful. In §[3.1,](#page-23-1) we give explicit detail on the plaquette operators and their connection to A-enriched skein modules. In §[3.2,](#page-32-0) we introduce the dome algebra Dome<sup>A</sup> (X ), communicated to us by Corey Jones and inspired by Kevin Walker, which is a quotient of Tube(X ) by an ideal J <sup>A</sup> coming from the A-enrichment. The point of Dome<sup>A</sup> (X ) is Theorem [3.9](#page-33-0) which shows that Rep(Dome<sup>A</sup> (X )) ∼= *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ), the enriched center [\[KZ18\]](#page-43-10) of X . We then show that our Tube(X )-action on the X string net model also gives an action on our Aenriched model which descends to an action of Dome<sup>A</sup> (X ). Our ideal J <sup>A</sup> acts as zero on Tube(X )-representations from precisely those string operators corresponding to anyons in Irr(*Z* <sup>A</sup>(X )), so localized enriched excitations exactly correspond to the enriched center *Z* <sup>A</sup>(X ) as claimed in [\[HBJP23\]](#page-43-9). Finally, in §[3.4,](#page-37-0) we analyze bulk excitations using the sphere algebra of A, again communicated to us by Corey Jones and inspired by Kevin Walker. Our analysis is consistent with [\[JFR24,](#page-43-2) Lem. 2.16]; the point excitations in the bulk are Ω*Z*(ΣA) = *Z*2(A). Under the additional assumption that the braided central functor Φ *Z* : A → *Z*(X ) is full, *Z*2(*Z* <sup>A</sup>(X )) = *Z*2(A) by [\[DNO13,](#page-42-6) Prop. 4.3], and the canonical map is exactly bringing a bulk excitation to the boundary via a modified hopping operator.

## <span id="page-3-0"></span>2 String net models for unitary fusion categories

Here we review the commuting projector Levin-Wen string net model built from a unitary fusion category (UFC) X , including the braided category of excitations and string operators. General references include [\[EGNO15\]](#page-42-7) for fusion categories, [\[HP17,](#page-43-13) [Pen20\]](#page-44-11) for UFCs, and [\[LW05,](#page-44-3) [Kon14,](#page-43-12) [LLB21\]](#page-44-5) for 2D Levin-Wen string net models. In particular, we always equip our UFC with its canonical unitary spherical structure, allowing us to form closed diagrams on a sphere which are invariant under isotopy.

We view these 2D string net models as condensing topological order from the vacuum, which is mathematically supported by [\[GJF19,](#page-43-1) [Mul22,](#page-44-4) [JF22\]](#page-43-5) and informs our diagrammatic calculus of shaded regions. Starting from the vacuum, from any UFC X one can attach a finite dimensional Hilbert space built from hom spaces in X to every vertex of a square lattice as in the first picture below. Enforcing the consistency condition that labels of simple objects on edges/links (the *A<sup>ℓ</sup>* terms) has the effect of condensing a 1-skeleton. Passing to the image of the plaquette/face operators (the *B<sup>p</sup>* terms) allows isotopy across each plaquette, effectively condensing 2-cells onto the 1-skeleton.

## <span id="page-3-1"></span>2.1 Skein modules and string nets

For this section, X is a unitary fusion category and Irr(X ) is the set of isomorphism classes of simple objects of X .

**Definition 2.1.** The disk skein module S<sup>X</sup> (D*, n*) for X with *n* boundary points is the Hilbert space orthogonal direct sum

$$\bigoplus_{x_1,\dots,x_n\in\operatorname{Irr}(\mathcal{X})} \mathcal{X}(1\to x_1\otimes\dots\otimes x_n) = \left\{ \begin{array}{c} x_1 \mid \dots \mid x_n \\ f \end{array} \right\}$$

$$\langle f|g\rangle := \frac{1}{\sqrt{d_{x_1}\cdots d_{x_n}}} f^{\dagger} \circ g = \frac{1}{\sqrt{d_{x_1}\cdots d_{x_n}}} \cdot \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix} f^{\dagger} \\ x_1 \mid \cdots \mid x_n \end{pmatrix}}_{g}.$$

Now as a  $\operatorname{Tube}(\mathcal{X}) - \operatorname{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$  bimodule, the regular representation  $\operatorname{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$  has one irreducible summand for each type of irreducible representation of  $\operatorname{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$ , i.e. each isomorphism class in  $\operatorname{Irr}(Z(\mathcal{X}))$ . We thus see that the anyon types of the two excitations at the punctures must agree, up to an opposite which occurs due to a left/right action of  $\operatorname{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$ , which translates into an anyon type and its dual at the end of the strings.

Now any local operator is localized away from at least one of the two excitations. While the local operator can destroy the  $\mathrm{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$  representation on one side, it is localized away from the other excitation, and therefore commutes with the action of  $\mathrm{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$  at the site of the other excitation. Hence the local operator still preserves the anyon type at the other puncture. So if our local operator takes one excitation to another at the same (or nearby) site, then the above argument says it cannot change the anyon type.

We conclude that on a twice-punctured sphere, an irreducible  $\mathrm{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$  representation type is a complete invariant of a superselection sector. It is invariant under applying local operators, and any finer invariant would necessarily split irreducible summands of the regular  $\mathrm{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$ -Tube( $\mathcal{X}$ ) bimodule, which disrespects the fact that  $\mathrm{Tube}(\mathcal{X})$  acts by local operators.

## <span id="page-23-2"></span><span id="page-23-0"></span>3 String net models for enriched unitary fusion categories

In this section we give a rigorous treatment of the enriched Levin-Wen string net model associated to an  $\mathcal{A}$ -enriched UFC  $\mathcal{X}$  from [HBJP23], where  $\mathcal{A}$  is a UMTC representing the Witt class of the anomaly. #### <span id="page-37-2"></span><span id="page-37-0"></span>3.4 Sphere algebra, bulk excitations, and bulk enrichment of boundary

Suppose  $\mathcal{A}$  is an arbitrary UBFC and  $\mathcal{X}$  is an  $\mathcal{A}$ -enriched UFC such that the unitary braided central functor  $\Phi^Z: \mathcal{A} \to Z(\mathcal{X})$  is fully faithful. In this section, we analyze the (3+1)D bulk  $\mathcal{A}$ -excitations and show they are  $Z_2(\mathcal{A})$  by constructing representations of the *sphere algebra* Sphere( $\mathcal{A}$ ), a further quotient of Tube( $\mathcal{A}$ ). ## 4 Conclusion

In this manuscript, we have reviewed the explicit construction of the 2D boundary boundary theory of the 3D Walker-Wang model given from [HBJP23] by a UMTC  $\mathcal{A}$  as a sort of enriched Levin-Wen model based on an  $\mathcal{A}$ -enriched UFC  $\mathcal{X}$ . Using techniques from TQFT, we have confirmed the prediction of [HBJP23] that the excitations of this 2D model are given by the enriched center/Müger centralizer  $Z^{\mathcal{A}}(\mathcal{X})$  of  $\mathcal{A}$  in  $Z(\mathcal{X})$ . We achieved this by defining the algebra Dome<sup> $\mathcal{A}$ </sup>( $\mathcal{X}$ ) and showing that its irreducible representations are in one-to-one correspondence with the excitations of the boundary theory. This is analogous to how the irreducible representations of the tube algebra Tube( $\mathcal{X}$ ) are in one-to-one correspondence of the excitations of an unenriched Levin-Wen model given by a UFC  $\mathcal{X}$ .

All of these arguments are made on finite lattices. There is also an operator algebraic approach [Naa17] for identifying excitation types on infinite lattices. It would be interesting to check that the excitation types on the infinite lattice align with those from our analysis.

In our construction, we have not considered protection by 0-form symmetries. A direction for future research would be to endow the bulk theory with such a symmetry and to study the excitations in the resulting anomalous G-enriched topological order in the 2D boundary theory.
