## Abstract

This thesis investigates whether there is a conformal field theory (CFT) corresponding to the Haagerup subfactor via microscopic lattice models in one and two dimensions. The even parts of every subfactor give rise to two Unitary Fusion Categories (UFCs), and the thesis studies quantum spin systems constructed from these categories to find connections to CFTs.

The Haagerup subfactor is the smallest subfactor with index larger than 4, making it the first interesting example requiring new techniques for building a CFT. The thesis investigates three approaches:
1. **Golden chain model** - A 1D spin chain for extracting CFT information
2. **Defect chains** - Spin chains with defects providing insight into UMTC construction
3. **Levin-Wen model** - A 2D lattice model yielding a topological quantum field theory

**Keywords:** Conformal field theory, anyon chains, fusion categories, Haagerup subfactor, Levin-Wen model

## Key Concepts

### Fusion Categories
- Simple objects, morphisms, tensor products
- F-symbols and pentagon equations
- Fusion multiplicities $N_{ab}^c$

### The Haagerup Subfactor
- Index: $\frac{5+\sqrt{13}}{2} \approx 4.30278$
- Three Morita-equivalent fusion categories: $\mathcal{H}_1$, $\mathcal{H}_2$, $\mathcal{H}_3$
- Trivalent description of $\mathcal{H}_3$

### Physical Models
- Anyon chains from UMTCs
- Defect chains and annular categories
- Levin-Wen string-net models
